How to Pay for GeoGuessr in Southeast Asia

Updated for common payment options
🇵🇭 Philippines
  • Debit/Credit cards (Visa/Mastercard)
  • GCash Mastercard or GCash Virtual Card → link to PayPal
  • Maya (formerly PayMaya) cards

Tip: If the site declines your card, try PayPal checkout or a virtual card (Wise/Revolut).

🇸🇬 Singapore
  • Cards (Visa/Mastercard/AMEX)
  • Apple Pay / Google Pay (when available)
  • PayPal as fallback

Straightforward: most bank cards work out of the box.

🇲🇾 Malaysia
  • Maybank, CIMB debit/credit cards
  • PayPal (popular & stable)

If 3‑D Secure fails, link your card to PayPal and retry.

🇮🇩 Indonesia
  • BCA, Mandiri, BNI cards
  • PayPal; some users link OVO/DANA to virtual cards

Virtual cards (Wise/Revolut) help if local cards get blocked.

🇹🇭 Thailand
  • Kasikorn, Bangkok Bank cards
  • PayPal widely accepted

Some use TrueMoney via virtual card → PayPal route.

🇻🇳 Vietnam
  • Vietcombank, BIDV cards
  • PayPal often the most reliable

Enable international online payments in your banking app.

Payment Troubleshooting

  • Turn on International / Online Payments in your bank app.
  • Ensure 3‑D Secure / OTP is enabled.
  • Try PayPal or a virtual card (Wise, Revolut) if direct card fails.
  • Use a billing address that matches your bank record.

Southeast Asia: Quick Clues

🇵🇭

Philippines

  • English + Tagalog/Visayan signage; “Barangay”, “Purok”.
  • Jeepneys, tricycles; concrete barangay arcs.
  • Left-hand drive traffic, but drive on the right side.
🇮🇩

Indonesia

  • Words like jalan (road), kota (city).
  • Black/white painted poles; license plates black with white text.
  • Drives on the left.
🇲🇾

Malaysia

  • Malay words; yellow diamond road signs common.
  • Palm oil plantations; kilometer markers with state codes.
  • Drives on the left.
🇹🇭

Thailand

  • Thai script; royal portraits; many 7‑Elevens.
  • Yellow center lines; bilingual highway signs.
  • Drives on the left.
🇻🇳

Vietnam

  • Latin alphabet with diacritics (e.g., Phố, Nguyễn).
  • Heavy motorbike presence; narrow shop-house streets.
  • Drives on the right.
🇸🇬

Singapore

  • Multilingual signs (EN/中文/MS/தமிழ்); clean, modern roads.
  • MRT signage; HDB blocks with block numbers.
  • Drives on the left.

Pro Tips to Improve Fast

  • Sun & Compass: Sun to the north suggests Southern Hemisphere (often Indonesia). Use the in‑game compass to orient.
  • Road Markings: Yellow center lines common in TH/SG; PH/MY often show white edge lines.
  • License Plates: ID: black plates white text; PH gov plates white with blue; SG white/yellow with clear fonts.
  • Architecture: Temples (TH), mosques with onion domes (MY/ID), Spanish-era churches (PH).
  • Vehicles: Tuk‑tuks (TH), jeepneys & tricycles (PH), motorbike swarms (VN/ID).
  • Language Sniping: Small words reveal a lot—ng clusters (PH), diacritics galore (VN), Thai curls (TH), Malay/Indo cognates (MY/ID).
  • Meta: Some regions have older/blurrier Street View coverage—adjust expectations for pinpointing.

Training Routine (15–20 mins)

  1. Warm‑up: 3 rounds on a SEA-only map with no moving (1 min each).
  2. Target practice: Pick one country map and do 5 locations with moving.
  3. Review: After each guess, scan signs/landmarks you missed; take notes.
  4. Speed round: 1 minute per location on mixed SEA.
  5. Cooldown: One country you struggle with (e.g., rural Thailand).

Track mistakes you repeat and turn them into flashcards.

Country Deep Dives

🇵🇭

Philippines

Right‑side traffic
  • Clues: Barangay markers, sari‑sari stores, Catholic monuments.
  • Roads: Mixed quality; concrete barangay roads are common.
  • Phone codes: 02 (Metro Manila), 32 (Cebu), 082 (Davao).
🇮🇩

Indonesia

Left‑side traffic
  • Clues: Jalan signs, mosques, kilometer posts with province codes.
  • Roads: Painted curb poles; rural volcanic soil landscapes in Java.
  • Plates: Region code prefix (e.g., B = Jakarta).
🇲🇾

Malaysia

Left‑side traffic
  • Clues: Yellow diamond warning signs; bilingual Malay/English.
  • Roads: Expressways with E‑numbers (e.g., E1, E2); toll plazas.
  • Architecture: Surau/mosques; shop-lots with uniform facades.
🇹🇭

Thailand

Left‑side traffic
  • Clues: Thai script; royal yellow flags; spirit houses at homes.
  • Roads: Route shields with white numbers on black.
  • Urban: Skytrain in Bangkok; dense signage clusters.
🇻🇳

Vietnam

Right‑side traffic
  • Clues: Words like Phố, Quốc lộ; red flags with yellow star.
  • Roads: Mileposts with QL (National Highways) numbers.
  • Urban: Dense wires, narrow alleys, cafe signage everywhere.
🇸🇬

Singapore

Left‑side traffic
  • Clues: HDB blocks with clear block numbers; ERP gantries.
  • Roads: Green expressway signs (AYE, PIE, CTE).
  • Urban: Clean sidewalks; bilingual EN/中文 signage common.

FAQ

Can I play GeoGuessr for free? ➕

Yes, but with limitations. GeoGuessr Pro unlocks unlimited play, competitive modes, party mode, and more maps.

What if my payment is declined? ➕

Enable international online payments and 3‑D Secure/OTP in your bank app, try PayPal, or use a virtual card (Wise/Revolut). Make sure your billing address matches your bank record.

What are the best maps for SEA practice? ➕

Start with a regional Southeast Asia map, then dive into country maps like Philippines Provinces, Java, Bangkok, and Singapore City. Mix no-move rounds with standard play.

Any ethical rules to remember? ➕

Avoid cheating (external locators or leaked seeds). GeoGuessr thrives on fair play and learning. Support the devs if you can.
